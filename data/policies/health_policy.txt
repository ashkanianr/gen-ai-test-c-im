MANULIFE HEALTH INSURANCE POLICY
Policy Number: HL-2024-001
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

================================================================================
SECTION 1: COVERAGE OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This policy provides comprehensive health insurance coverage for eligible medical expenses incurred by the policyholder and covered dependents. Coverage includes but is not limited to hospital stays, physician services, diagnostic tests, prescription medications, and emergency medical care.

Coverage Period: Annual coverage from January 1 to December 31, renewable annually.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: COVERED SERVICES
================================================================================

Section 2.1: Emergency Medical Services
Emergency medical services are covered when provided at a hospital emergency room or urgent care facility. This includes treatment for acute conditions such as broken bones, severe injuries, heart attacks, strokes, and other life-threatening conditions. Coverage includes emergency room visits, diagnostic tests, and immediate treatment required to stabilize the patient.

Section 2.2: Preventive Care
Routine preventive care services are fully covered, including annual physical examinations, routine dental cleanings (up to two per year), vaccinations, and health screenings. Preventive care services are covered at 100% with no deductible or copayment required.

Section 2.3: Prescription Medications
Prescription medications prescribed by a licensed physician are covered when included in the formulary. Covered medications include both generic and brand-name drugs when medically necessary. Prior authorization may be required for certain specialty medications.

Section 2.4: Telemedicine Services
Telemedicine consultations with licensed healthcare providers are covered for routine check-ups, follow-up visits, and non-emergency consultations. Telemedicine services are subject to the same copayment as in-person visits.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: MEDICAL PROCEDURES AND TREATMENTS
================================================================================

Medically necessary procedures and treatments are covered when prescribed by a licensed physician. This includes surgical procedures, diagnostic imaging (X-rays, MRIs, CT scans), laboratory tests, and therapeutic treatments. Coverage is subject to prior authorization for procedures exceeding $5,000 in cost.

Section 3.1: Hospitalization
Inpatient hospital stays are covered for medically necessary treatment. Coverage includes room and board, nursing care, medications administered during hospitalization, and all medically necessary services provided during the stay.

Section 3.2: Outpatient Services
Outpatient services including same-day surgery, diagnostic procedures, and therapeutic treatments are covered when medically necessary and performed at an approved facility.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: REIMBURSEMENT AND CLAIMS
================================================================================

Claims for covered services must be submitted within 90 days of the date of service. Reimbursement will be processed according to the policy's fee schedule and subject to applicable deductibles and copayments.

Section 4.1: Claim Submission
Claims may be submitted electronically through the member portal or by mail. Required documentation includes itemized bills, receipts, and any supporting medical records requested by the insurer.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

The following services and conditions are NOT covered under this policy:

- Cosmetic surgery and procedures performed solely for aesthetic purposes
- Experimental or investigational treatments not approved by regulatory authorities
- Services related to weight loss programs unless medically necessary for treatment of obesity-related conditions
- Services for which the policyholder is not legally obligated to pay

================================================================================
SECTION 6: ACTIVITY AND LIFESTYLE EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

Section 6.1: Extreme Sports and High-Risk Activities
Medical expenses resulting from participation in extreme sports or high-risk activities are NOT covered. This includes but is not limited to: bungee jumping, skydiving, base jumping, extreme skiing, professional racing, and participation in combat sports without proper safety equipment.

Section 6.2: Elective Cosmetic Procedures
Any procedure performed primarily for cosmetic purposes, including but not limited to facelifts, breast augmentation, liposuction, and rhinoplasty, is excluded from coverage unless required for reconstructive purposes following an accident or medical condition.

================================================================================
SECTION 7: PRE-EXISTING CONDITIONS
================================================================================

Pre-existing conditions diagnosed or treated within 12 months prior to the policy effective date are excluded from coverage for the first 12 months of the policy. After the 12-month waiting period, pre-existing conditions may be covered subject to the policy's standard terms and conditions.

Section 7.1: Definition of Pre-Existing Condition
A pre-existing condition is defined as any medical condition, illness, or injury for which the policyholder received medical advice, diagnosis, care, or treatment within the 12 months preceding the policy effective date.

Section 7.2: Chronic Condition Management
Chronic conditions that existed prior to policy inception are subject to the pre-existing condition exclusion period. Ongoing management of chronic conditions may be covered after the exclusion period expires.

================================================================================
SECTION 8: DURABLE MEDICAL EQUIPMENT
================================================================================

Durable medical equipment (DME) prescribed by a licensed physician is covered when medically necessary for the treatment of a covered condition. This includes wheelchairs, hospital beds, oxygen equipment, and other devices as specified in the DME coverage list. Prior authorization is required for equipment exceeding $1,000 in cost.

Section 8.1: DME Coverage Criteria
Durable medical equipment is covered when: (1) prescribed by a licensed physician, (2) medically necessary for treatment of a covered condition, (3) not primarily for comfort or convenience, and (4) obtained from an approved DME supplier.

================================================================================
SECTION 9: EXPERIMENTAL AND INVESTIGATIONAL TREATMENTS
================================================================================

Experimental or investigational treatments are NOT covered. A treatment is considered experimental if it: (1) lacks sufficient peer-reviewed research demonstrating safety and efficacy, (2) is not approved by relevant regulatory authorities for the specific condition, or (3) is part of a clinical trial without proper coverage authorization.

================================================================================
SECTION 10: INTERNATIONAL COVERAGE
================================================================================

Emergency medical services received outside the policy's service area may be covered on a case-by-case basis. Prior authorization is strongly recommended for planned international travel. Coverage for international medical expenses is limited to emergency conditions that cannot wait until return to the service area.

Section 10.1: International Emergency Coverage
Emergency medical treatment received abroad is covered when: (1) the condition is truly emergent and cannot be delayed, (2) treatment is received at a licensed medical facility, and (3) the policyholder contacts the insurer within 48 hours of the emergency.

================================================================================
SECTION 11: SPECIALTY SERVICES AND EQUIPMENT
================================================================================

Specialty medical services and equipment may require prior authorization. Coverage determinations for specialty services are made based on medical necessity, evidence-based guidelines, and policy terms. The policyholder may be required to provide additional medical documentation for specialty service claims.

================================================================================
SECTION 12: ACTIVITY-SPECIFIC EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

Medical expenses resulting from participation in the following activities are excluded: professional sports, extreme sports (as defined in Section 6.1), illegal activities, and activities performed while under the influence of alcohol or drugs (unless the policyholder is the victim of a crime).
