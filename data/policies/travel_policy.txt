MANULIFE TRAVEL INSURANCE POLICY
Policy Number: TR-2024-001
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

================================================================================
SECTION 1: COVERAGE OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This policy provides comprehensive travel insurance coverage for trips taken by the policyholder. Coverage includes medical emergencies, trip cancellation, trip interruption, lost luggage, and travel delays. Coverage is valid for trips of up to 90 days in duration.

Coverage Territory: Worldwide coverage excluding countries under travel advisories issued by the policyholder's home country government.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: TRIP CANCELLATION AND INTERRUPTION
================================================================================

Trip cancellation coverage applies when a trip must be cancelled before departure due to covered reasons. Trip interruption coverage applies when a trip must be interrupted after departure due to covered reasons.

Section 2.1: Covered Cancellation Reasons
Covered reasons for trip cancellation include: (1) illness or injury of the policyholder or immediate family member requiring medical attention, (2) death of the policyholder or immediate family member, (3) natural disasters at the destination, (4) terrorist incidents at the destination, and (5) jury duty or court subpoena that cannot be postponed.

Section 2.2: Excluded Cancellation Reasons
Trip cancellation is NOT covered for: (1) change of mind or personal preference, (2) financial circumstances of the policyholder, (3) business obligations (unless specifically covered under business travel rider), (4) pre-existing medical conditions known at time of booking, and (5) travel to destinations under government travel advisories.

Section 2.3: Reimbursement Limits
Trip cancellation and interruption benefits are limited to the non-refundable portion of prepaid travel expenses, up to the policy maximum of $10,000 per trip.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: MEDICAL EMERGENCIES ABROAD
================================================================================

Emergency medical treatment received outside the policyholder's home country is covered when the condition is truly emergent and cannot wait until return home. Coverage includes hospital stays, physician services, emergency medical evacuation, and necessary medications.

Section 3.1: Emergency Medical Coverage
Emergency medical expenses are covered when: (1) the condition requires immediate medical attention, (2) treatment is received at a licensed medical facility, (3) the condition is not a pre-existing condition (unless covered under pre-existing condition waiver), and (4) the policyholder contacts the assistance hotline within 48 hours of the emergency.

Section 3.2: Medical Evacuation
Medical evacuation to the policyholder's home country or nearest appropriate medical facility is covered when medically necessary as determined by the attending physician and the insurer's medical director. Coverage includes air ambulance services, medical escort, and related transportation costs.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: LOST, STOLEN, OR DAMAGED LUGGAGE
================================================================================

Coverage is provided for personal belongings that are lost, stolen, or damaged during the trip. Coverage applies to checked luggage, carry-on items, and personal effects.

Section 4.1: Coverage Limits
Luggage coverage is limited to $1,500 per person per trip. High-value items such as jewelry, electronics, and cash are subject to lower sub-limits as specified in the policy schedule.

Section 4.2: Documentation Requirements
Claims for lost or stolen luggage must be supported by: (1) police report (for theft), (2) airline or transportation provider report (for lost luggage), (3) itemized list of lost items with estimated values, and (4) receipts or proof of ownership for items valued over $500.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: TRAVEL DELAYS
================================================================================

Coverage is provided for additional expenses incurred due to travel delays caused by covered reasons. Covered reasons include: weather conditions, mechanical failure of transportation, strikes, and natural disasters.

Section 5.1: Delay Coverage
Travel delay benefits are payable when a covered delay causes the policyholder to incur additional expenses for accommodations, meals, and transportation. Benefits are payable after a minimum delay of 6 hours.

Section 5.2: Reimbursement Limits
Travel delay benefits are limited to $200 per day, up to a maximum of $1,000 per trip. Receipts are required for all claimed expenses.

Section 5.3: Missed Connections
Coverage is provided for additional expenses incurred due to missed connecting flights or transportation when the delay is caused by a covered reason and results in additional accommodation or transportation costs.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: TRIP CANCELLATION EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

Trip cancellation coverage does NOT apply to cancellations due to: (1) change of mind or personal preference, (2) financial circumstances, (3) business obligations (unless covered under business travel rider), (4) fear of travel, (5) travel to destinations under government travel advisories issued after policy purchase, and (6) pre-existing medical conditions known at time of booking (unless covered under pre-existing condition waiver).

Section 6.1: Personal Preference Exclusions
Cancellations made solely due to the policyholder's change of mind, preference, or convenience are not covered. This includes cancellations due to finding a better deal, scheduling conflicts that could have been anticipated, or simply deciding not to travel.

================================================================================
SECTION 7: FAMILY EMERGENCY COVERAGE
================================================================================

Trip cancellation or interruption due to family emergencies is covered when the emergency involves an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or sibling) and requires the policyholder's presence or prevents travel.

Section 7.1: Covered Family Emergencies
Covered family emergencies include: (1) serious illness or injury requiring hospitalization, (2) death, (3) natural disasters affecting the family member's residence, and (4) other emergencies as determined on a case-by-case basis.

Section 7.2: Documentation Requirements
Family emergency claims must be supported by medical documentation, death certificate, or other appropriate documentation demonstrating the emergency and its impact on the trip.

================================================================================
SECTION 8: PERSONAL EFFECTS AND VALUABLES
================================================================================

Coverage for personal effects extends to items carried by the policyholder during the trip. High-value items are subject to sub-limits and may require additional coverage through a valuables rider.

Section 8.1: Valuables Sub-Limits
The following items are subject to reduced coverage limits: jewelry ($500), electronics ($1,000), cameras and photographic equipment ($750), and cash ($200). Items exceeding these limits require separate coverage.

================================================================================
SECTION 9: PRE-EXISTING CONDITIONS
================================================================================

Pre-existing medical conditions are excluded from coverage unless: (1) the condition is stable for 90 days prior to trip departure, (2) no medical treatment was required for the condition in the 90 days prior to departure, and (3) a pre-existing condition waiver was purchased at the time of policy purchase.

Section 9.1: Stability Requirement
A condition is considered stable when: (1) no new symptoms have appeared, (2) no changes to medication or treatment were made, (3) no medical consultations were required, and (4) no hospitalizations occurred related to the condition.

================================================================================
SECTION 10: CLAIMS PROCESSING
================================================================================

All claims must be submitted within 90 days of the incident or return from trip, whichever is later. Claims must include all required documentation as specified in the policy and claims guide.

Section 10.1: Required Documentation
Required documentation varies by claim type but generally includes: claim form, receipts, medical records (for medical claims), police reports (for theft claims), and any other documentation requested by the insurer.
